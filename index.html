About the Group

A simple idea: teach kids a few fun line dances, then go brighten someone else‚Äôs day.

The Howdy Boot Line-Dancers is a volunteer-led group where an instructor donates time to teach kids the basics of line dancing. Most dancers are elementary-age girls, but all kids are welcome as space allows.

Practices are relaxed, kid-paced, and focused on listening, teamwork, and respect. Dancers learn that effort matters more than perfection, and that a friendly smile can be the best part of the performance.

Once kids feel comfortable with a few routines, the group visits nursing homes and community events to share a short, upbeat show. It‚Äôs a gentle way for kids to practice being brave, kind, and outward-focused.
